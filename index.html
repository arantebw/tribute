Dennis MacAlistair Ritchie

The C programming language creator, UNIX OS co-developer, and
"The C Programming Language" book co-author

Caveat: This site is a work in progress.

Timeline

1941

  • He was born on September 9, 1941 at Bronxville, New York, USA.

1967

  • He joined Bell Labs corporation that was "jointly owned by American Telephone and Telegraph Company and its subsidiary Western Electric", following the footsteps of his father.

1968

  • The subject of his doctoral thesis was "Subrecursive Hierarchies of Functions".

1974

  • He received the ACM award for the outstanding paper of 1974 in systems and languages, in conjunction with Ken Thompson.

1982

  • He received the IEEE Emmanuel Piore Award, in conjunction with Ken Thompson.

1983

  • He became a Bell Laboratories Fellow, in conjunction with Ken Thompson.
  • He received the Association for Computing Machinery Turing Award, in conjunction with Ken Thompson.
  • He received the ACM Software Systems Award, in conjunction with Ken Thompson.

1984

  • He was now identified as working for AT&T Bell Laboratories.

1988

  • He was elected to the U. S. National Academy of Engineering, in conjunction with Ken Thompson.

1989

  • He received the C&C Foundation award of NEC, in conjunction with Ken Thompson.

1990

  • He received the IEEE Hamming Medal, in conjunction with Ken Thompson.

1999

  • He received received the U. S. National Medal of Technology, in conjunction with Ken Thompson.

2005

  • The Industrial Research Institute awarded Ritchie its Achievement Award in recognition of his contribution to science and technology, and to society generally, with his development of the Unix operating system.
  • The Computing Sciences Research Center of Bell Labs was re-organized and his team was re-distributed the he became of a member of a new team, namely Convergence, Software and Computer Science Laboratory.

2011

  • He was awarded the Japan Prize for Information and Communications for his work in the development of the Unix operating system.
  • He died at the age of 70.
