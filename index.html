Dennis MacAlistair Ritchie

The C programming language creator, UNIX OS co-developer, and
"The C Programming Language" book co-author

Caveat: This site is a work-in-progress.

Timeline

1941

  • He was born on September 9, 1941 at Bronxville, New York, USA.

1967

  • He joined Bell Labs corporation that was "jointly owned by American Telephone and Telegraph Company and its subsidiary Western Electric", following the footsteps of his father.

1968

  • The subject of his doctoral thesis was "Subrecursive Hierarchies of Functions".

1984

  • He was now identified as working for AT&T Bell Laboratories.

2005

  • The Industrial Research Institute awarded Ritchie its Achievement Award in recognition of his contribution to science and technology, and to society generally, with his development of the Unix operating system.
  • The Computing Sciences Research Center of Bell Labs was re-organized and his team was re-distributed the he became of a member of a new team, namely Convergence, Software and Computer Science Laboratory.

2011

  • Awarded the Japan Prize for Information and Communications for his work in the development of the Unix operating system.
  • Died at the age of 70.
